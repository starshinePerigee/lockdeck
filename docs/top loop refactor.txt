Another simplification to consider: changing the top level loop to use a "turns" format.

- At start of turn: draw up to N cards
- Play as many as you'd like
- Once all cards are played, any unlocked cylinders fall all the way

What this does:
- removes the "bounce" mechanic
- significantly simplifies the "fall" mechanic
- (possibly) removes the unlock depth's bind-on-unlock mechanic
- removes the "discard pick" mechanic
- makes jam mechanic way more valuable

What this needs:
- some kind of time pressure
-- note: time pressure also means picks with more icons become more valuable

Time pressure brainstorming:
- break pick on end of turn (from discard?)
- clock that counts down to failure?
-- shape it like a wheel?
-- shape it like a candle burning down?
- sudden death modes (YOU ARE OUT OF TIME)
-- every pick breaks
-- picks that break BREAK FOR GOOD
--- could lead to degenerate play where you thin your deck by playing out time
-- permanently break a pick at end of turn
-- picks break end of turn at random
