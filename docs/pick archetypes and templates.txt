What pick archetypes are useful?
- bulk push - move lots of pins in safety / emergency
- light push to close out a position
- tall push to skip danger
- mid push filler pick
- broad hints to give you basic outline
- tall hints to line up safe skips after reveal
- narrow hints to identify threats conclusively
- small jams for tricks
- large jams for protection of cleared pins
- reveal and crush to deal with upcoming threats
-- reveal + hint + jump = safe
-- crush is useful as a defense against particularly difficult layouts


Templates:

pick templates:
- name
- effect
- rarity - common, uncommon, rare.
- value - in coins
- family
- two graphics: pick and base

Rarer templates arent necessarily better, just weirder
All templates should be somewhat flawed and unweildy. A good pick needs to be crafted at least a little bit.



RAKES - affect many pins. typically have a mix of push and test
- bulk push - peak rake
- broad test - snake rake
- mid test - worm rake

DIAMONDS - affect a few pins a little bit, also have a mix of push and test
- mid push - half diamond
- light push - small diamond

HOOKS - navigate a danger via push, test, and reveal
- tall push - gem hook
- narrow test - short hook
- tall test - reach hook
- reveal - soft hook

JAMS - used for tricks and safekeeping
- split - fork
- small jam - light ball
- large jam - heavy ball

CRUSHES - used to deal with really hard combos
- crush - lever


when balancing, note that high push pins are way more likely to break. a jam/jam pick *cant* break through normal means but a 12-push is basically good for two uses
